Hi there! Nice to meet you!

I am Ashad Ahmad, a passionate PhD researcher at the Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad, in the field of Astronomy and Astrophysics, working with Dr. Sunil Chandra. Previously, I completed my Masters in Astronomy from the Indian Institute of Technology Indore (IIT Indore), where I worked under the supervision of Dr. Bhargav Vaidya.

I primarily work on Active Galactic Nuclei (AGNs), particularly blazars, which are laboratories of extreme physics in the Universe. My research interests lie in high-energy astrophysics and neutrino astronomy, focusing on how relativistic jet environments in blazars accelerate particles to extreme energies and also on the multi-messenger emission from these relativistic jets. In my PhD, I study particle acceleration at relativistic shock fronts and its connection to observable signatures such as radio and X-ray variability and the possible production of high-energy neutrinos.

In addition to theoretical work, I am also an observational astronomer at the Mount Abu Observatory, where I have conducted optical photometric and polarimetric observations of blazars using the 1.2m telescope and the 2.5m telescope, using instruments such as the Faint Object Camera (FOC) and EMPOL (an EMCCD based optical imaging polarimeter).
