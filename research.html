Research

Spectral Energy Distributions (SEDs) of blazars, which are a type of active galactic nuclei (AGN) with relativistic jets pointed towards Earth, show a characteristic two-hump structure with the low-energy peak typically lying between radio and optical/UV/X-rays, while the high-energy peak extends from X-rays to GeV–TeV γ-rays (see Figure (i)). The low-energy hump is generally attributed to synchrotron radiation from relativistic electrons spiralling in the jet's magnetic field, while the origin of the high-energy hump is often explained by inverse Compton scattering of low-energy photons by the same population of electrons or by hadronic processes involving relativistic protons and their interactions with photons (photohadronic interactions).

My work focuses on investigating the SEDs of blazars by coupling the various particle acceleration mechanisms such as diffusive shock acceleration (DSA) or magnetic reconnection, with the radiation processes to understand the observed SEDs of blazars.
